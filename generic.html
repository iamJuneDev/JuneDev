Jurisprudence & Law

This interdisciplinary approach of law and politics critizes legal and political implications considering the Syrian civil war case study that triggered American diplomatic interference. Primary sources for the premise of this analysis include Executive Branch documents, presidential statements, executive orders, as well as congressional materials, House and Senate resolutions, and federal laws.

International legal materials, comparatively the 1998 Rome Statute of the International Criminal Court are examined along side with investigative reports that reveals cracks in the foundation of the peacemaking through the ICC. The political aspects of this topic are integrated with legal and jurisdictional concerns of international law. The question is raised on whether the ICC should be given jurisdiction and diplomatic support for investigating the crimes against humanity commited by the regime.
